# Arts Tasmania Grant Application 2026
## Organisation Funding for New Projects

**Applicant Organisation:** Secret Lab  
**Application Date:** 2026  
**Funding Category:** Organisation Funding for New Projects  
**Focus:** Hobart-based Arts Development

---

## Executive Summary

Secret Lab is applying for organisation funding to support new arts-focused projects that will strengthen Hobart's cultural landscape and creative sector. Our proposed initiatives emphasise locally-based arts development, community engagement through creative practice, and the advancement of innovative artistic expression within Tasmania's capital city.

This application seeks funding to establish and deliver programs that will:
- Foster artistic excellence and innovation in Hobart
- Create opportunities for local artists and creative practitioners
- Engage the community through accessible, high-quality arts experiences
- Contribute to the cultural vibrancy and economic development of Hobart

## Project Proposal

### Project Overview: Hobart Arts Innovation Initiative

Our proposed project centres on developing and delivering arts programs that are deeply rooted in Hobart's unique cultural identity while pushing creative boundaries.

### Project Objectives

1. **Cultivate Artistic Excellence**
   - Support the development of new artistic works by Hobart-based artists
   - Provide resources and opportunities for creative experimentation
   - Foster innovation in artistic practice and presentation

2. **Strengthen Community Connections**
   - Create accessible arts experiences for Hobart residents
   - Build bridges between artists and audiences
   - Develop inclusive programs that reflect community diversity

3. **Enhance Hobart's Cultural Profile**
   - Position Hobart as a centre for arts innovation
   - Attract attention to local artistic talent
   - Contribute to the city's cultural tourism appeal

4. **Build Sustainable Arts Infrastructure**
   - Establish ongoing support systems for artists
   - Create pathways for emerging creative practitioners
   - Develop partnerships with local venues and organisations

### Project Activities

#### Core Program Components

**1. Artist Development Programs**
- Residencies for Hobart-based artists
- Mentorship and professional development opportunities
- Access to facilities and resources for creative practice
- Showcasing opportunities for completed works

**2. Public Arts Events**
- Exhibitions featuring Hobart artists
- Performances in accessible community venues
- Interactive arts experiences for diverse audiences
- Collaboration with local cultural organisations

**3. Community Engagement**
- Workshops and educational programs
- Artist talks and panel discussions
- Open studio events and behind-the-scenes access
- Youth arts initiatives

**4. Documentation and Promotion**
- Professional documentation of artistic works
- Marketing and promotion of Hobart arts scene
- Digital presence showcasing local creativity
- Media engagement and publicity

### Hobart-Centric Approach

This project is fundamentally Hobart-focused:

- **Local Artist Priority:** All programs will prioritise Hobart-based artists and creative practitioners
- **Community Integration:** Activities will be embedded in Hobart neighbourhoods and cultural spaces
- **Place-Based Content:** Artistic works will engage with Hobart's unique environment, history, and identity
- **Local Partnership:** Collaboration with Hobart venues, organisations, and community groups
- **Economic Impact:** Direct economic benefits to Hobart's creative sector and local businesses

### Arts-Focused Excellence

Our commitment to arts excellence includes:

- **Artistic Integrity:** Maintaining high standards in all creative outputs
- **Innovation:** Encouraging experimental and boundary-pushing artistic practice
- **Diversity of Forms:** Supporting various artistic disciplines and cross-disciplinary work
- **Critical Engagement:** Fostering meaningful dialogue around artistic practice
- **Professional Development:** Building capacity within the arts sector

## Risk Management

### Identified Risks and Mitigation

**Artist Availability**
- Risk: Key artists may be unavailable
- Mitigation: Build robust artist pool, maintain waiting list, flexible scheduling

**Community Engagement**
- Risk: Lower than expected participation
- Mitigation: Extensive marketing, diverse program offerings, free/low-cost access

**Venue Access**
- Risk: Challenges securing appropriate venues
- Mitigation: Early booking, multiple venue relationships, flexible program design

**Financial Management**
- Risk: Budget overruns or funding shortfalls
- Mitigation: Conservative budgeting, regular monitoring, contingency fund

**External Factors**
- Risk: Unforeseen disruptions (weather, health issues, etc.)
- Mitigation: Flexible programming, insurance, contingency planning
